Understanding Bayes' Theorem

What It Is Why It Matters Misconceptions Real Life Takeaways FAQ References

1. What It Is

Bayes' Theorem describes how to update beliefs when new evidence appears.

2. Why It Matters

It protects us from base rate neglect and irrational overreaction.

3. Common Misconceptions

Bayesian thinking is not subjective guesswork — it's structured updating.

4. Real-World Applications

Medical testing, spam filtering, courtrooms, investing, AI systems.

5. Practical Takeaways

  • Always check base rates
  • Update gradually
  • Demand strong evidence for big shifts

6. FAQ

Who created Bayes’ Theorem? Thomas Bayes.
What is a prior? Your belief before evidence.
What is a posterior? Your belief after updating.
Is Bayes used in AI? Yes, extensively.
